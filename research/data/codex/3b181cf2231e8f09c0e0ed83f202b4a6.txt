Students at Vols basketball game protest University of Tennessee's blackface responseJim Gaines and Mike Wilson, Knoxville Published 9:01 p.m. ET March 5, 2019 | Updated 8:45 p.m. ET March 6, 2019CLOSE Students at Tennessee basketball game protest UT's blackface response WochitCONNECTTWEETLINKEDINCOMMENTEMAILMOREProtesting the University of Tennessee's handling of a blackface controversy, 40 to 50 students dressed in black occupied seats behind UT's goal Tuesday night at the men's game against Mississippi State.At the start of the game, they remained seated during the national anthem as other students, clad in orange and white, stood. Sitting or kneeling during the anthem has become a standard method of protesting racial injustice since 2016, following the example of football player Colin Kaepernick.Breaking news, at your fingertips.Download our free app today.Students protest during the National Anthem before Tennessee's home finale against Mississippi State at Thompson-Boling Arena in Knoxville on Tuesday, March 5, 2019. (Photo: Brianna Paciorka/News Sentinel )During the first half, the assembled students employed a variety of chants aimed at Tennessee."Hey, hey, ho, ho, racism has got to go," they chanted."No justice, no peace, no racist UT," they chanted another time."What do we want? Expulsion! When do we want it? Now!" they yelled at halftime."Which side are you on, UT? Which side are you on?" was another chant.Students protest during the National Anthem before Tennessee's home finale against Mississippi State at Thompson-Boling Arena in Knoxville on Tuesday, March 5, 2019. (Photo: Brianna Paciorka/News Sentinel )The majority of the students left early in the second half, most of them holding their right fists in the air as they walked up the aisle.Help us power local journalism.Become a digital subscriber.Vice Chancellor for Student Life Vince Carilli said Monday afternoon, at a meeting with students and UT officials, that the university was still deciding how to discipline students who appeared in blackface in a Snapchat image last week. He said it was unlikely they would be expelled. Calls to Carilli on Wednesday were directed to UT director of media relations, Tyra Haag. Haag, also contacted Tuesday evening for comment, did not return calls.In the image, two people appear to wear black skincare masks.CLOSE UT has released a statement condemning images reportedly posted by students that show them wearing blackface and captioned with racist comments.
