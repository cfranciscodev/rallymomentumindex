They chanted "Black lives matter" and "I can't breathe," making reference to PROVIDENCE, R.I. -- Several hundred people took a meandering path through downtown Friday night, stopping at several intersections to sit down in a protest against the grand jury decision in New York not to charge the police officer in the chokehold death of Eric Garner.There were no reports of arrests or violence.The marchers left from the area of Central High School on Westminster Street about 6:45 p.m., and marched over the Route 95 overpass by McVinney Auditorium. There were reports that more than one of the marchers descended at least partway down the embankment to the highway, but there were no reports that any of the protesters got to the highway. Just over a week ago, several people were arrested after they blocked the southbound lanes during a demonstration linked to the Ferguson, Mo., grand jury decision.The crowd, monitored by police, made its way to the Providence Public Library on Empire Street and sat down in the street. After a short period, they moved down Washington Street to Dorrance Street and then to Francis Street and the Providence Place mall. Police blocked any of the protesters from going inside the mall.Just over an hour after they began, the crowd turned around at the mall and headed back to Central High School just before 8 p.m.[video-protest]Protests are taking place Friday around the country:-- In New York City, a group of more than 100 protesters lapped Manhattan's Columbus Circle on Friday, then walked to an Apple store, where they lay on the floor for a few minutes as a symbolic die-in. They proceeded down sidewalks through Fifth Avenue's renowned shopping district, carrying signs and chanting "Black lives matter" and "I can't breathe."-- In White Plains, N.Y., about 65 people including a 3-year-old girl lay down as if dead Friday on a suburban street corner to protest the chokehold death of an unarmed black man at the hands of a white New York City police officer. Some of the protesters wore hospital-type masks over their mouths with "We can't breathe" written on them.-- In Chicago, dozens of protesters gathered Friday afternoon at a downtown intersection before meandering through the area. They headed to Federal Plaza and stopped near Chicago Public Schools headquarters before holding a brief rally at the city's official Christmas Tree. Friday's was a smaller demonstration than that late Thursday, when hundreds of protesters blocked Lake Shore Drive.-- In Florida, activists marched through the streets of midtown Miami and blocked a major causeway connecting Miami to Miami Beach.-- In Colorado, about 400 students from several Aurora high school and middle schools converged on the Denver suburb's municipal center.Authorities temporarily locked down the court building, city hall and library during the protest. The protest was largely peaceful, but police said some students banged and spit on patrol cars.About 150 students also protested at the state Capitol as officers stood watch on the West Steps.-- In the Boston area, demonstrators rallied for a second-straight night Friday.The police say a crowd that grew to about 1,000 people marched from Somerville to Cambridge and blocked traffic on several streets including Massachusetts Avenue. Several hundred people lay down in Harvard Square. No violence was reported.Social media posts indicated the march was organized by students at Tufts University.-- In New Haven, Conn., hundreds of people including Yale Law School students rallied. Demonstrators marched Friday afternoon from the law school to New Haven Superior Court, where they lay down on a street in a "die-in" protest. There were no reports of arrests.-- In Madison, Wis., dozens of protesters have marched through the city. Organizers of Friday's protest stressed that their grievances go beyond those two cases, and involve broader concerns about what they consider police brutality against African-Americans.-- In New Brunswick, N.J., dozens of students from Rutgers University marched through the city, which was forced to postpone its tree-lighting ceremony.The protests also slowed downtown rush hour traffic to a crawl as they temporarily blocked traffic on Route 18 and Easton Avenue.The protesters laid down in front of the New Brunswick police department building.[video_dec5_protest_pvd]-- with reports from John Hill, Journal staff writer, and the Associated Press Never miss a story Choose the plan that's right for you.
